Our Flagship Operation

BGI Resources is our primary operated asset: a 35-well oil production portfolio spanning three field areas across Osage County, Oklahoma, with exclusive drilling concessions covering over 100 square miles.

BGI acquired the field in 2001 when it was producing just 8 barrels of oil per day. We invested in 3D seismic interpretation to understand the subsurface and entered a drilling farm-in agreement in 2008, bringing in a partner to help fund development. BGI took over as operator in 2012, significantly cutting costs and increasing production through disciplined field management.

Growth & Consolidation

Over the years following our 2012 takeover as operator, BGI systematically grew the portfolio through workovers, recompletions, and infill drilling. In 2025, the company purchased back the remaining farm-in interests through its related entity Mustang Colt, consolidating 100% working interest across the field.

Both entities, BGI Resources and Mustang Colt, are managed by Berkeley Geoimaging, LLC. Together they hold a complete ownership position with the Osage Mineral Estate as the single mineral owner and a 3/16 overriding royalty.

Operations Today

Our expertise is in shallow vertical wells drilled to approximately 3,000 feet total depth. These wells offer a high level of operational control, predictable production curves, and manageable decline rates. All drilling and prospect activity is controllable from our field office, and new wells come online at marginal cost due to existing infrastructure and stable base production.

2025 Gross Production: ~44,000 barrels
Working Interest: 100% (BGI Resources + Mustang Colt)
Average Well Cost: ~$400K per well

Prospect Portfolio
